# Unemployment Analysis in India During COVID-19

## Project Overview
This project analyzes unemployment trends in India using Python. The analysis focuses on understanding unemployment patterns, investigating the impact of COVID-19, identifying seasonal trends, and generating insights that can support economic and social policy decisions.

## Objectives
- Clean and preprocess unemployment data.
- Explore unemployment trends over time.
- Analyze the impact of COVID-19 on unemployment rates.
- Compare rural and urban unemployment.
- Identify regions with high unemployment rates.
- Provide policy recommendations based on findings.

## Technologies Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings
- Average unemployment increased from 9.51% before COVID-19 to 17.77% during the pandemic.
- Urban unemployment rates were higher than rural unemployment rates.
- Tripura and Haryana recorded the highest average unemployment rates.
- April and May 2020 experienced the sharpest rise in unemployment.

## Policy Recommendations
- Strengthen employment guarantee schemes.
- Support small businesses during crises.
- Promote skill development initiatives.
- Develop urban employment programs.

## Dataset
The dataset contains unemployment statistics across various Indian states from 2019 to 2020.
